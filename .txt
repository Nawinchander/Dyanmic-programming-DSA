Dynamic Programming (DP) in Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) is a technique used to solve complex problems by breaking them into smaller overlapping subproblems and storing the results so we don’t recompute them again.


1. Overlapping Subproblems

The same subproblems are solved multiple times.

Example:
Fibonacci calculation repeatedly calculates the same numbers.


2. Optimal Substructure

The optimal solution can be built from optimal solutions of smaller problems.
